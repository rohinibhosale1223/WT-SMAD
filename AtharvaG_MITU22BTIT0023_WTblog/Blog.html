TOP 06 TREKS OF MAHARASHTRA

Peace is on the top of the hill



1.KALSUBAI PEAK


The Kalsubai Trek is the most sought-after and is regarded as the highest peak of Maharashtra to trek upon.

Considered a moderate-level trek it's situated at 5400 feet above sea level.

The locals think that Kalsubai is named after a small woman named Kalsu who worked as a housemaid in a nearby village. She was known for her healing abilities and generosity. She helped many villagers in her short lifespan. Villagers narrate that she disappeared into the meadows one day and never returned to the village. It is believed that Kalsubai breathed her last at the summit hence it became a place of worship for the locals.

2.HARISHCHANDRA GAD


The Harishchandragad fort is one of the most thrilling and challenging treks in the state of Maharashtra, taking in the grandeur at a height of 4668 feet.

The fort is mentioned in the descriptions of ancient writings like Matsyapurana, Agni Purana, and Skanda Purana. The fort was built in the sixth century when the Kalchuri dynasty governed the area.

According to legend, the Sage Changdev pondered in the caverns in the fourteenth century. In 1747 AD, the Mughals and Marathas fought bitterly for control of this fort. In the excavations, prehistoric human remains have also been discovered.

3.NANEMACHI WATERFALL


Nanemachi Waterfalls is one of the best waterfalls in Maharashtra. This waterfall is located at a distance few kilometers from Mahad. Nanemachi waterfalls are a natural attraction inside a dense forest.

The place is famous for its blue water that flows inside the waterfall. It has a depth of about 400 feet and is well hidden.

Nanemachi waterfall pool glows in different shades of color throughout the day, which adds to its majestic beauty.

4.HARIHAR FORT


The Maharashtra district of Nashik is home to the Harihar fort. It is located 3,676 feet above sea level.

From the base town, the fort's hill appears to have a rectangular shape. However, it is based on a triangular prism of granite that dates back to the Yadava dynasty. The Harihar fort's exterior walls are nearly vertical.

At the fort, there are statues of various Hindu Deities and a small pond.

5.RAJGAD FORT


The most picturesque hike in Maharashtra is the Rajgad trek. The Fort is a center of attraction for eons due to its fascinating history and stunning construction. Itâ€™s one of a kind fort in the Western Ghats.

The Maratha ruler Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj resided in Rajgad for 26 years before relocating to Raigad. This is one of the factors that contributed to the fort's exquisite construction.

It is located at a height of 4,514 feet above sea level. The fort was constructed on a hill named Murumb Devacha Dongar and has a 40 km diameter when measured. The fort's enormous size made it difficult to lay siege to it, which allowed the Marathas to plan their strategy.

6.BHIMASHANKAR TREK


A lover of nature will adore the Bhimashankar trip. As you follow the trail through the dense forests, it opens its heart and welcomes you with open arms into the Bhimashankar Wildlife Sanctuary.

With stream crossings, ladders, and rock climbing all rolled into one hike, the Bhimashankar trek provides you with a full dose of adventure.

This trek can be done in other seasons, but the monsoon is the ideal time to undertake it. The path is exciting and picturesque. Trekking enthusiasts can summit the Bhimashankar by taking the Shidi Ghat route. While the Ganesh Ghat route, which has beautiful vistas and is simpler, is advised for novices.
